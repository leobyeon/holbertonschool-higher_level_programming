the ``text_indentation`` function
==================================
using ``text_indentation``
--------------------------
import function:
	>>> text_indentation = __import__('5-text_indentation').text_indentation

some tests:

	empty string:
	>>> text_indentation("") 

	triple quotes:
	>>> text_indentation("""Lorem: ? \n""")
	Lorem:
	<BLANKLINE>
	?
	<BLANKLINE>
	<BLANKLINE>

	space colon space:
	>>> text_indentation(" : ")
	:
	<BLANKLINE>

	single question mark:
	>>> text_indentation("?")
	?
	<BLANKLINE>

	newline and space:
	>>> text_indentation("\n ")
	<BLANKLINE>

	space newline dot:
	text_indentation(" \n.")
	<BLANKLINE>
	.
	<BLANKLINE>

	ellipsis:
	text_indentation("...")
	.
	<BLANKLINE>
	.
	<BLANKLINE>
	.
	<BLANKLINE>

	newline space a space bc and lots of spaces:
	text_indentation("\n a bc     ")
	<BLANKLINE>
	a bc
